Book 1: The First Shrine
Kit: afkshrine_books_starter
Pages: 8 of 20

Page 1
Nobody remembers who
went AFK first.

Some say it was a
builder with soup on
the stove.

Page 2
They stood near a hill
on 1MoreBlock, pickaxe
in hand, staring at a
half-finished path.

Then life called.

Page 3
The sun moved.
The grass waved.
The sheep judged the
path very quietly.

Still, the player did
not move.

Page 4
When they returned,
tiny lights circled
their boots.

Not loud.
Not flashy.
Just enough to say:
welcome back.

Page 5
They checked their
pockets.

No diamonds had
appeared.
No magic sword.

Only a small feeling
that time had noticed.

Page 6
So they claimed their
tokens, stretched their
hands, and fixed one
crooked stair.

That was enough.

Page 7
By evening, others had
seen the glow.

"I was only getting
tea," someone said.

"The shrine waited,"
said another.

Page 8
That is how the first
AFK Shrine began.

Not as a reward for
leaving,
but as a soft hello
when you return.

Book 3: The Keeper Who Waited
Kit: afkshrine_books_keeper
Pages: 9 of 20

Page 1
There is a keeper in
the quiet corners of
1MoreBlock.

No one has seen them.
Everyone has returned
to their work.

Page 2
The keeper does not
carry a sword.

They carry a lantern,
a notebook, and a
very patient smile.

Page 3
When a player goes AFK,
the keeper sits nearby.

They count clouds.
They count steps not
taken.
They count the seconds
kindly.

Page 4
If rain falls, the
keeper writes:

"Rainy watch.
Boots wet.
Player safe."

Page 5
If night comes, the
keeper writes:

"Stars bright.
No panic.
Probably making
sandwich."

Page 6
If the player returns,
the keeper closes the
book.

The shrine lights up.
The bossbar hums.
The world continues.

Page 7
The keeper never asks
why you left.

Doorbell?
Dinner?
Homework?
Real life?

All valid.

Page 8
They only ask what you
will do next.

Claim your tokens?
Check a milestone?
Try a new preset?
Finish the roof?

Page 9
The keeper waited.

Now you are back.

Go make the server a
little more yours.


Book 4: Rainwatch Journal
Kit: afkshrine_books_weather
Pages: 8 of 20

Page 1
Rainwatch Journal

Found beside a spruce
tree after a long
storm on 1MB.

The pages smell a bit
like wet wool.

Page 2
It started raining
right after I went AFK.

I had gone to close a
window.

Minecraft also closed
the sky for me.

Page 3
When I came back, the
shrine had noticed the
rain.

It felt like the world
had kept a little
weather diary.

Page 4
Thunder rolled once.

My dog barked in real
life.
My wolf in-game sat
perfectly still.

Good wolf.

Page 5
The return postcard
said where I had been,
what I had seen, and
why it counted.

For a chat message, it
felt oddly cozy.

Page 6
I claimed my tokens,
then checked the mud
near the farm.

Somehow rain makes
even unfinished builds
look planned.

Page 7
If you go AFK in rain,
listen when you return.

The drops may be gone,
but the shrine remembers
the storm.

Page 8
Note to self:

Build a covered bench
near the wheat field.

AFK breaks deserve
good seating.


Book 5: Under The Idle Stars
Kit: afkshrine_books_weather
Pages: 8 of 20

Page 1
At night, the server
gets quieter.

Not silent.
Never silent.

Just soft enough to
hear your own plans.

Page 2
I went AFK on a roof
while looking at the
stars.

I meant to be gone for
one minute.

Classic mistake.

Page 3
When I came back, the
moon had moved.

The shrine was active.
The bossbar had changed.

My roof was still
missing a corner.

Page 4
The postcard said I
was under open sky.

It also said I took no
damage.

That is rare roof
behavior, honestly.

Page 5
I claimed my tokens,
checked my streak, and
placed the missing
corner block.

Progress is progress.

Page 6
Some players chase
dragons.
Some sort chests.
Some return from AFK
and remember why they
were on a roof.

All are heroes.

Page 7
If you read this under
the stars, take a
moment.

Look around.
Find one thing to
finish when you return.

Page 8
The night is not empty.

It is full of builds
waiting, farms growing,
and tiny shrine lights
saying:

welcome back.


Book 6: The Biome Traveler's Pause
Kit: afkshrine_books_travel
Pages: 9 of 20

Page 1
I packed bread,
torches, and one boat.

Then I forgot the boat
at home.

So began my biome
adventure.

Page 2
Forest first.

I went AFK beside an
oak tree because real
life asked where I put
the laundry.

The tree knew nothing.

Page 3
Desert next.

I paused near a cactus,
which felt brave until
I remembered cacti are
not furniture.

Do not lean.

Page 4
Ocean after that.

The shrine liked the
view.
I liked that I was not
underwater.

The boat was still at
home.

Page 5
In each place, the
return postcard told a
small story.

Biome.
Weather.
Why it counted.
What was new.

Page 6
That made me want to
try another place.

Not because I had to.
Because the world is
large, and my map was
too empty.

Page 7
The album remembered
where I had been.

It felt less like a
checklist and more like
a travel journal with
better spelling.

Page 8
If you always AFK in
one room, that is cozy.

But once in a while,
take your break where
the view is different.

Page 9
Then return.
Claim your tokens.
Mark the biome.

And maybe, finally,
remember the boat.


Book 7: Safe Harbor Logbook
Kit: afkshrine_books_keeper
Pages: 8 of 20

Page 1
Safe Harbor Logbook

For players who build
AFK corners with doors,
lamps, snacks, and
too many signs.

Page 2
Rule 1:

Solid ground is good.

The floor should not
be lava, cactus, or
one suspicious trapdoor.

Page 3
Rule 2:

A roof is optional,
but confidence is not.

If phantoms can see
you, perhaps add a
ceiling.

Page 4
Rule 3:

Leave a chair.

You cannot sit in it
while AFK, but your
character deserves the
thought.

Page 5
When you return, read
the postcard first.

It may say you were
safe, dry, undamaged,
and wonderfully boring.

This is a compliment.

Page 6
Claim your tokens.
Check your rewards.
Then fix one small
thing in the room.

A lantern.
A flowerpot.
A better sign.

Page 7
Safe places are not
lazy places.

They are little harbors
between adventures.

Even heroes need to
answer the door.

Page 8
If you found this book
in a kit, your harbor
is official now.

Please keep it cozy.
Please label the
storage.


Book 8: Wild Rest Field Notes
Kit: afkshrine_books_travel
Pages: 9 of 20

Page 1
Wild Rest Field Notes

Written by someone who
went AFK outside and
lived to explain why
that was risky.

Page 2
The first rule of wild
rest is simple:

Look around before you
leave.

Then look around again.

Page 3
I once went AFK in a
flower field.

Peaceful.
Pretty.
Full of bees.

No issue, but it felt
like a meeting.

Page 4
Another time, I paused
near a cave mouth.

The postcard later
mentioned open sky and
danger.

It was polite about it.

Page 5
The shrine does not
tell you to be reckless.

It notices the story
your location tells.

That is different.

Page 6
If you return to find
no tokens, read why.

Maybe the session was
too short.
Maybe the place was
too familiar.

Try again later.

Page 7
If you return to find
progress, nice.

Claim it.
Smile at the postcard.
Do not brag to the
zombie nearby.

Page 8
Wild places make good
memories.

Bring blocks.
Bring food.
Bring common sense.

And maybe a friend.

Page 9
The best AFK spot is
not always the safest.

But the best return is
the one where you know
what happened.

The Token Ledger

Property of nobody,
because everyone kept
borrowing it.

Please return after
counting shiny things.

Page 2
Entry:

One AFK Shrine Token
found after a very
important sandwich
break.

Value: sentimental.

Page 3
Entry:

Two tokens traded for
something mysterious.

The player said,
"Worth it,"
then ran to their base.

Page 4
Entry:

Three notes, one token,
and a name tag were
seen together.

This probably means an
upgrade is coming.

Page 5
Reminder:

Pending tokens are not
the same as claimed
tokens.

Claim first.
Spend later.
Panic never.

Page 6
The ledger does not
judge how long you
were gone.

It only records what
the shrine allowed and
what you chose to do.

Page 7

tiny stories.

A spear improved.
A shield remembered.
A book opened.
A note spent.

Page 8
Final entry:

The richest player is
not the one with the
most tokens.

It is the one who came
back and used them.
